‘Rate’ Your Mood

The Rate tab allows you to rate your mood. T2 Mood Tracker comes pre-programmed with six mood categories: Anxiety, Depression, General Well-Being, Head Injury, Post-Traumatic Stress, and Stress. You can create custom mood categories by tapping + Add Custom Rating Category at the bottom of the Rate screen.

To rate a mood category, tap the category name. This takes you to a screen populated with sliders. The left end of the slider shows a negative feeling, and the other end shows a positive feeling. Simply move the slider toward the end that best reflects how you currently feel. Stronger feelings should be rated more to the extreme edges, while milder feelings will trend closer to the middle.

When you are done with your ratings, touch save to return to the Rate tab. Your rating is automatically included on the graph for that mood category.

On the Rate tab (and any other tab), you can also add a note by touching Add Note on the top right corner of the screen.

Reviewing Your ‘Results’

The Results tab allows you to view all of your ratings by date, manage reports, and review your previous notes.

Graph Results

Selecting Graph Results takes you to a graph showing your previous ratings. When in portrait view, the graph provides multiple configuration options. When in landscape view, the graph takes up the entire screen to make viewing results easier. Each point on the graph represents the average of your ratings for that mood category for that specific date and time. Data points toward the top of the graph represent higher levels of each mood category (high Anxiety, high General Well-Being) while ratings toward the bottom of the graph represent lower levels.

Use pinch and zoom to narrow and increase your data views, and simply swipe the graph left or right to pan through the data points.

Customizing Your Results

Sharing results is easy! Touch the Share button in the upper right corner to save or email a screenshot of your current graph, or to save it as an image to your Photo Gallery.

Customizing the color & symbols used in the graph – Tapping on the symbol to the left of a category takes you the Select Color view. Select a color then touch the Next button. This brings up a symbol selection screen. Select the desired symbol then hit Save. You can also customize how the individual feelings within a mood category appear by selecting the name of the category while in the Graph Results screen, and follow the same process.

When in portrait view, the Categories, Notes, and Options buttons allow you to configure the graph how you want it to look.

Categories – This button allows you to control which categories you see on the graph. Flip each category’s switch On or Off to update the graph appropriately. Tap a category name to see the individual negative/positive scales for that category. This allows you to view the graph for the individual feeling scales within one mood category. Like the category view, flipping a switch On or Off will update the graph appropriately. To return to the category view, tap Back in the top left corner.

Notes – This button shows your saved notes starting with the most recent. Tap a specific note to see a full-screen view of the note. From here, you can update the note with additional information, or touch the Back button to return to the Graph Results view. If you make changes to a note, touch Save in the top left corner.

Options – This button allows you to configure many options on the graph. Like the other views, using the On/Off switches will turn those options on and off.

  • Symbols add random symbols to the graph lines to help better distinguish the category data. You can customize these symbols and the color options as well.
  • Gradient adds a shaded gradient to the category data lines.
  • Data Range allows you to select the default data range the graph results show. Use the selector to choose 30 days, 90 days, 180 days, or 1 Year. This restricts the data shown on the graph to the range selected. These options should be experimented with if you have a large amount of data points and want to speed up the chart generation process. You can access data beyond 1 year by exporting the data to a CSV or PDF report.

Create Reports

Selecting Create Reports brings up a report generator. From here, you can specify exactly what data to include in a report.

  • Date Range will define the start and end point for the data you include. Tap either Start Date or End Date, and use the date selector. Notes determines whether or not to include your notes with the report. Use the On/Off switch to control that output.
  • Categories allows you to select which categories to include in the report. Again, use the On/Off switch to control that output.

When you have all the parameters set, select the save button in the top right corner. This gives you two options: Save as CSV, or Save as PDF.

  • Save as CSV exports the data range selected to a CSV formatted file and saves it on your device. You can share this file from the Saved Reports screen, where you can select and review your saved reports.
  • Save as PDF exports the data range selected to a PDF formatted file and saves it on your device. PDF reports are easy to read and include a spark chart of each category, as well as the scale information for each category. Linear regression trend lines are included to help determine the overall trend of the data. Using the Share icon in the top right, you can email the report to your provider, or to whomever you choose. Please keep in mind that email is not secure, and even though your report contains no personal identifying information, the data you send can still be linked back to you by your email address.

Saved Reports

This button takes you to the Saved Reports page where you can view your saved reports. To email a PDF report, tap the report and select Email PDF. To view a PDF, tap the report and select View PDF. Selecting a CSV file will automatically prompt you to send it via email. Select the confirmation button if you’d like to send it, and cancel if you don’t.

Pressing a report name for a long hold, or swiping left/right on the report, gives you the option to delete that specific report. Be careful with this option, as all deletions are permanent unless you have that report saved elsewhere.

View Notes

This button shows your saved notes starting with the most recent. You can add another note by touching the + symbol at the top right, or you can tap a specific note to see a full-screen view of the note. From here, you can update the note with additional information, or touch the Back button to return to the Graph Results view. If you make changes to a note, touch Save in the top left corner.

Pressing a note for a long hold, or swiping left/right on the note, gives you the option to delete that specific note. Be careful with this option, as all deletions are permanent.

‘Support’ Functions

The Support tab provides some communication and resource-location options.

About T2 Mood Tracker provides background information on why T2 Mood Tracker was created and the National Center for Telehealth & Technology.

Help links you to this help file (so we guess you already found it!)

Feedback opens an email with our email address. Send us your feedback so we can continue improving T2 Mood Tracker.

Rate App links to the marketplace where you downloaded T2 Mood Tracker. Rate the app and provide some feedback for other potential users.

‘Settings’

The Settings tab takes you to the heart of customizing T2 Mood Tracker. Within Settings, you can add and edit categories, customize graphs, setup reminders, enable security, turn startup tips on or off, and clear old data.

Add/Edit Rating Categories

This button takes you to the Rating Categories screen. This area contains the six pre-programmed categories included in T2 Mood Tracker. These pre-programmed categories cannot be deleted or modified, but you can choose whether or not they display in your Rate tab area by toggling the On/Off switch for each category.

To add a new category, touch the + button in the top right corner. This will take you to the New Category screen. From here, you can add a category name and choose whether the category is positive in nature. If you accidentally indicate that a negative trait, such as sadness or anger, is positive, it will display incorrectly in your graphed results.

In the Rating Scales pane, add your own customized scales (you won’t be able to use the new category until you populate it with at least one scale). Tap the + button, and you’ll be queried to use an existing scale (one already built by your, or used in the pre-programmed categories), or to create a new one.

If selecting from an existing scale, simply scroll down until you find the one you want to use, and tap on it. If creating a new scale, fill in what the undesirable and desirable traits are, e.g., Sad/Happy, then choose the save button when satisfied. Continue this process until satisfied with the scales for your new category. You can add up to 10 different scales for a category. If you’d like to remove a scale, simply use the left/right swipe, or long hold option to delete it.

To edit an existing category, tap on the category. You can rename it, change the negative trait feature, add new scales, etc. To delete the category, press Trash Can icon on the top right. You will receive a confirmation message to complete the deletion.

Reminders

You can set up to three different reminders a day so you don't forget to make your ratings.

Security

This area allows you to choose a PIN to secure T2 Mood Tracker on your device and protect your data. When you select your PIN, you establish two security questions so you can recover your PIN if you forget it. You can deactivate this feature by inputting your current PIN and then clearing the function.

Clear Data

This allows you to clear all ratings and/or notes from a category. Tap on a category or the notes section to clear the data. You will receive a confirmation message to complete the deletion. If you choose Ok, all the data from that category will be deleted. Be careful with this option, as all deletions are permanent.

Send Anonymous Data

Knowing how T2 Mood Tracker is being used helps the developers modify and upgrade the app based on anonymous usage patterns. This information DOES NOT compromise your personal anonymity in any way. You may disable this function by selecting Off.

Show Startup Tips

This allows you to turn the health tips on the initial splash screen On or Off. You can also disable the startup tips on the initial splash screen.
